I was fortunate enough to travel to Argentina last year on April. It was my first time going to South America! I cannot state enough how amazing it was to travel to a new continent and really immerse myself in a new culture and environment

Why I Picked Argentina

I first decided on going to Argentina after my friend threw out the idea. At the time, she left her job and had time before her new job started (2 months I believe). She wanted to spend that time solo backpacking across South America! She wanted me to join her, but I unfortunately would not be able to join for a trip longer than two weeks since I had work. Since she was following the Andes mountains from Peru to Argentina, I decided to join her in her last leg of the trip, in North West Argentina!

Travel

I decided to fly Delta, as they offered a non stop flight from JFK to EZE (International Airport in Buenos Aires). I was nervous I would not be able to sleep in the flight to Buenos Aires, but I ended up knocking out for most of the flight (thankfully!!!). Now this is when it gets fun. I needed to take a flight from the other airport in Buenos Aires to meet my friend in San Salvador de Jujuy, the capital of the Jujuy province. This was my first time in Argentina, and my Spanish was extremely limited... Thankfully I speak italian which is super close to Spanish, so I was able to converse with some taxi drivers and get a taxi ride form one airport to the next, all the while stopping at a Western Union to get some cash.

Argentinian Pesos

Cash in Argentina was definitely a hot commodity. Considering inflation was about 1000% in the past 5 years, most Argentinians held their cash in currencies other than the local Argentinian Pesos, and then exchanged it at Western Unions for a "black market" exchange rate, which was much lower than the actual exchange rate. Great for tourists because we got insane value for the dollar, not great for the locals at all however. Western Unions are known to run out of cash, leaving a lot of Argentinians high and dry. As of now, May 2025, inflation has plunged to 66.9% though, so things are improving!

The bus system in rural Argentina is very nice, so we were able to travel around with a bus. We decided to rent a car for a Salta⟹Cafayate⟹Cachi road trip, but other than that we relied on mostly public transportation
